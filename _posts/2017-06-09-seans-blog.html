Making a decent blog

Last week we began working with Javascript, this week we moved to AngularJS. It's purpose is to facilitate the Javascript, but I don't know enough about either yet to make and educated post about them. However, I did learn how to add a Favicon to my blog. It is the tiny little image that shows next to the title of your page on the browser tab. As you can tell, it looks pretty badass which is fitting for such a blog. Let's go thru the steps:


First, pick an image you want to turn into the favicon. I picked an image of a diamond. Just by googling 'favicon', you get a bunch of results for favicon generators. Here is the generator I used. On the choose file button, upload your image and hit the 'Create Favicon' button. Boom. As a side note, I had all the 'Maintain Image Dimensions' option selected the first time I tried and it had a big white space underneath the image. I tried again with that option unchecked and it came out how I intended.(


Next, click the link to 'download generated favicon'. Save the image to the file you want and open your text editor. The download opens up a list of options for the format of your favicon. However, your favicon must match the dimensions of 32x32 or 16x16 to work. I found the image labeled favicon-32x32.png and renamed it for easy reference. I created folder specifically for images on my blog, which makes them easy to find. Drag and drop your renamed favicon into that image folder.


In the head section of your html document, paste the following code:


<link rel="shortcut icon" href="/images/diamond/diamond.png" type="image/x-icon">


'/images/diamond' refers to where I have the image stored. I saved all the generated images into a 'diamond' folder. 'diamond.png' is what I named the specific 32x32 favicon.


Open in your browser, or refresh your page. Kaboom. Boomshakalaka. There it is.
